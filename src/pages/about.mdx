<div className={styles.bioSection}>

With an insatiable curiosity, I devote a large part of my life to programming, exploring open source, and sharing knowledge, whether through projects, articles, or exchanges within tech communities.

For years, I have been actively contributing to the Joomla ecosystem, both in Belgium and in the [French-speaking](https://forum.joomla.fr/member/38299-cavo789) world: participating in the organization of [JoomlaDays](https://www.joomladay.fr/?view=article&id=56:christophe-avonture&catid=17), writing articles for the [Joomla Community Magazine](https://magazine.joomla.org/), developing extensions now obsolete (SportingEvents & AllEvents) and sharing tutorials.

In addition, Docker has played a central role in my daily life for the past four years. I enjoy popularizing its use: creating tutorials, leading sessions, but also building reproducible environments so that developers can work more efficiently. I also explore modern tools such as Dagger.io to refine and automate my CI/CD workflows.

As far back as I can remember, even as a kid, I have kept this quote in mind:

<div className={styles.blockquote}>
  "Go back to your work twenty times: polish it constantly and polish it again;
  add something now and then, and often erase."
  <br />
  <small>
    — Nicolas Boileau / L'Art poétique · *"Vingt fois sur le métier remettez
    votre ouvrage : Polissez-le sans cesse et le repolissez ; Ajoutez
    quelquefois, et souvent effacez"*
  </small>
</div>

This is, for me, the most important thing in being a developer: never stop striving to do better, and if you have to refactor your code, just do it — right now. I would also add this:

<div className={styles.blockquote}>
  It goes without saying that code should work, but it must also be clean,
  optimized, well-formatted and documented, and refactored every time you can.
  Code should always reflect the best of your skills.
</div>

</div>
